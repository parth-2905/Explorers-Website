Rumtse to Tso Moriri Trek

An 8-day trek through the stunning Changthang region of Ladakh

Day 1: LEH - RUMTSE

Drive from Leh to Rumtse and stay overnight to acclimatise to the high altitude. Day free to relax and do short hikes around.

Time 4 hrs
Elevation Gain +740m
Elevation Loss 0m

Day 2: RUMTSE - KYAMAR

Follow the trail to Kyamar which was once known as the salt road. Through this valley, the salt from Tso Kar was transported to Leh and the Indus valley. The sceneries and landscapes are different from the rest of Ladakh.

Time 4 hrs
Distance 10.9 km
Elevation Change +298m / -0m

Day 3: KYAMAR - KYAMAR LA - MANDALCHAN LA - TISALING

Today trek towards the Kyamar La and enjoy the fantastic view of the Changthang region from the pass, then climb down towards Tiri Doksa, a temporary shelter for the shepherds. Then climb the Mandalchan La and then climb down to Tisaling.

Time 6-7 hrs
Distance 13.5 km
Elevation Change +786m / -384m

DAY 4: TISALING - SHIBUK LA - PONGANAGU

Today we will slowly begin to gain elevation towards the Shibuk La pass from where you will get the spectacular view of Tso Kar Lake from the pass. Then walk down to Ponganagu near Tso Kar Lake where we camp for the night. This area is famous for wildlife such as migratory birds, kiang (wild asses), marmots and ibex.

Time 6 hrs
Distance 14.3 km
Elevation Change +377m / -666m

DAY 5: PONGANAGU - NURUCHAN

Today continue the trek walking along the west side of TsoKar lake where you might be able to spot black necked cranes and brahmini ducks. Tso Kar is also called "white lake" because of the salt that get deposited on its banks. Form there we trek till to Nuruchan where we will set up camp for the night.

Time 5-6 hrs
Distance 17.8 km
Elevation Change +173m / -145m

DAY 6: NURUCHAN - KYAMAYURI LA - GYAMAR BARMA

Today we will be crossing two passes (Horlam Kongka La and Kyamayuri La) to reach Gyamar Barma. On the way, you will meet several nomad families with their yaks, sheep, and goats.

Time 7-8 hrs
Distance 19.3 km
Elevation Change +855m / -339m

DAY 7: GYAMAR BARMA - KARTSE LA - GYAMAR

After crossing the Kartse La, go down towards the Gyamar valley where we camp for the night.

Time 3 hrs
Distance 5.7 km
Elevation Change +213m / -251m

DAY 8: GYAMAR - YALUNG NYAU LA - KORZOK

We will cross Yalung Nyau La, the highest pass of the trek and from the pass you will have a great view of Tso Moriri lake. Walk down to Korzok village, on the bank of Tso Moriri.

Time 5-6 hrs
Distance 16.3 km
Elevation Change +300m / -905m

TRAVEL TIPS

Acclimatization

Most of Ladakh and areas beyond Sarchu is at an altitude of more than 3000m so you need to get acclimatized before you can set out to travel. Your body needs to adapt to high altitude, as the higher you go, the air becomes rarified and the oxygen available for you to breathe becomes lesser. Even though your body can adapt to 'thin air', you need to give it time to do so. This is what is called acclimatization.

Acclimatization is essential to avoid acute mountain sickness (AMS). The symptoms include headache, lethargy, dizziness, and loss of appetite. You must allow a full day of complete bed rest for acclimatizing to the high altitude and low level of oxygen. High altitude sickness or Acute Mountain Sickness (AMS) is a serious condition that can happen to anyone at altitudes above 3000 meters or 10000 feet.

Keep yourself hydrated all the time, drink water and don't experiment with Alcohol on first two days.

HEALTH

Travel to any part of the Himalayas deserves a little more respect than many other destinations because the whole region lies over 3000 meters. People in good health should not get alarmed by this but if you have a medical condition such as high blood pressure, heart, or lung disease, you must take the advice of a doctor who has experience with the effects of altitude. We do not take heart or lung patients or pregnant mothers on this expedition. Any kind of exercise which gets you fitter before this trip is advisable, as it will enable you to enjoy more.
