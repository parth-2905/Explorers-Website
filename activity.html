Exploring The Dark Skies - Ladakh Stargazing

Embark on a celestial journey with our Dark Sky Tours in Ladakh, where the night skies are among the darkest on Earth. These nocturnal expeditions unravel the mysteries of the cosmos, offering immersive discussions on astronomy, awe-inspiring views of celestial bodies, and the opportunity to capture stunning images of the universe through telescopes and cameras.

Ladakh's elevation, ranging from 3,500 to 5,000 meters, provides an unparalleled vantage point for observing the heavens. Imagine a celestial spectacle with minimal atmospheric interference, offering crystal-clear views of the twinkling stars rated on the Bortle dark-sky scale.

Dark Sky Viewing Site at Shey, Ladakh

We have a dedicated dark sky viewing site in Shey, meticulously chosen for the ultimate night sky experience. With over five years of expertise in stargazing in the region, we identified this site as the most suitable location in Nubra Valley, offering the widest, uninterrupted views of the night sky.

On moonless nights, the site features a Bortle 1 class sky, providing unparalleled visibility of the cosmos. Through our world-class astronomy equipment, you can easily observe star clusters, deep space objects such as galaxies and nebulae, and even the Zodiacal Light, which spans the entire night sky.

This panoramic view is unique to our dark sky site in Shey, where prominent mountain rangesâ€”the Stok and Ladakh Rangesâ€”create a stunning natural backdrop in the northern part of the Indian subcontinent.

What You'll See

1. A Panoramic Canvas

Witness the Milky Way, a mesmerizing band of millions of stars stretching across the sky, in all its glory.

2. Constellations

Identify familiar constellations like Orion, Ursa Major, Cassiopeia or spot your own zodiac also discover new ones.

3. Planetary Parade

Gaze at planets like Jupiter, Saturn, and Mars, their moons visible with a telescope. Witness the rings of Saturn, the bands of Jupiter with your own eyes.

4. Nebular Delights

Marvel at colourful nebulae, like the Ring Nebula, NGC 6563 and many more.

5. Meteors

Witness the fleeting beauty of meteors streaking across the sky.

6. Meteor Showers

Witness the mass Streaking of meteor across the night sky during special events like the Perseid Meteor Shower, every month offers a different meteor shower with varied levels of concentration.

7. Comets

These interstellar visitors are a rarer sight and if you're lucky you might spot one during certain periods of the year.

Dark Sky Site

The Dark Sky site is located near the River Indus with magnificent views. Shey village is situated at an altitude of around 3,600 meters (11,800 feet) and offers stunning vistas of the surrounding mountains and valleys. The village was once the capital of Ladakh and holds a unique place in the region's history and culture. One of the main attractions of Shey is the Indus River and the beautiful sunset view from the banks of the river.

Zanskar River Rafting Expedition

Experience the Adventure

The Zanskar River Rafting is an epic adventure quite unlike anywhere else in the world. It provides the best opportunity to enjoy and experience the natural beauty of the spectacular landscape with deep gorges, towering snowcapped peaks, hilltop monasteries, hillside villages, and glimpses of the unique wildlife. The trip begins with a flight over the Himalayas in the Trans-Himalayan region of Ladakh. We spend a few days in Leh town, acclimatizing at 11000 feet above sea level and visiting the ancient monasteries, palaces and villages around Leh.

We drive west out of Leh past the town of Kargil and travel south into the valley of Zanskar, one of the coldest inhabited places on Earth. Starting the trip on the Doda River, we begin our self-contained multi-day river adventure down the spectacular and scenic Zanskar river gorge. The trip takes you down the extremely desolate, remote and sheer Zanskar gorge with walls rising a few thousand feet out of the river bed, culminating on the mighty Indus River at the historic monastic conclave at Alchi.

Practical Information

Temperatures

In the Himalayas, temperatures can change at any moment. During daylight hours the temperature can be as high as 25 degrees and at night it can dip down to 10 degrees OR below zero if the weather is overcast.

What to Carry?

Good Footwear:

Good strap on sandals works well on the rafts as well as at camp. They dry quickly as well so it also saves the trouble of carrying alternate footwear. If you are carrying only sandals, make sure they are in good shape to last the 5 days on water.

Clothing:

A good base layer which could be a thermal top (polypropylene) with a T-shirt on top will keep you warm and dry. Mid layers for camp provide insulation. Any good waterproof, windproof jacket would do the job. Leg wear in the form of thermal long johns are invaluable. Cotton trousers or long skirts worn over this layer can keep you very comfortable. A good sun hat is very essential. Sunglasses which offer 100% UV projection are necessary. On the rafts, T-shirts, shorts are best. We will provide splash jackets, or even wetsuits if weather gets bad.

A good quality sleeping bag ensures a good night's sleep after a long day outdoors.

Medical:

Carry any personal medication that you may need, or let us know should you be suffering from any particular aliment.

What we Provide?

Our crew of guides will also be supplemented by a cooking crew, which would prepare the days meals for the days we camp out. Cuisine is decided keeping in mind the profile of the group.

Please keep your personal items to a minimum so that your bags are light and easy to carry.

Trekking in Ladakh

Trekking in Ladakh is a unique and unforgettable experience that combines natural beauty, adventure, and cultural richness. Nestled in the northernmost region of India, Ladakh is known for its rugged mountains, high-altitude passes, and stunning landscapes. Popular trekking routes like the Chadar Trek (on the frozen Zanskar River), Markha Valley Trek, and Stok Kangri Trek attract adventurers from around the world.

The region's stark beautyâ€”featuring snow-capped peaks, barren deserts, and crystal-clear lakes like Pangong and Tso Moririâ€”makes every trek visually rewarding. Trekkers also get a chance to interact with the warm and hospitable Ladakhi people and explore ancient monasteries perched on mountain cliffs.

Due to the high altitude, physical fitness and proper acclimatization are essential. Summer (June to September) is the best time for trekking, as the weather is favorable and the routes are open. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced trekker, Ladakh offers a magical journey through one of the most breathtaking terrains on Earth.

Popular Treks in Ladakh

Kanji to Pishu Trek

Experience the remote beauty of Ladakh's hidden valleys on this challenging trek that connects the villages of Kanji and Pishu.

  • Duration: 7-8 days
  • Difficulty: Moderate to Challenging
  • Best Season: July to September

Markha Valley Trek

One of the most popular treks in Ladakh, offering stunning views of Kang Yatse and Stok Kangri peaks, along with traditional villages and monasteries.

  • Duration: 6-7 days
  • Difficulty: Moderate
  • Best Season: June to September

Rumtse to Tso Moriri Trek

A challenging high-altitude trek that leads to the beautiful Tso Moriri Lake, crossing several passes and remote landscapes.

  • Duration: 8-9 days
  • Difficulty: Challenging
  • Best Season: July to September

Sham Valley Trek

Perfect for beginners and those short on time, this trek offers beautiful landscapes, ancient monasteries, and traditional Ladakhi villages.

  • Duration: 3-4 days
  • Difficulty: Easy to Moderate
  • Best Season: May to October
